Why Does My Phone Need HTTPS?

Short answer: Your phone's browser refuses to use the microphone on websites that don't use HTTPS (the padlock icon). This is a security rule baked into iOS and Android — no app or setting can override it.

When you open Dictator on your phone, it's a web page served by your PC. Normally local web pages use plain HTTP, but browsers block microphone access on HTTP pages to prevent malicious sites from recording you.

Dictator solves this by serving the page over HTTPS with a security certificate. The desktop app runs two HTTPS servers and shows you two URLs — pick whichever suits your situation:

LAN — Port 3456 (Self-Signed Certificate)

  • Always available — runs every time you start Dictator, no setup needed
  • Same WiFi only — your phone and PC must be on the same network
  • Browser warning once — your phone will show a security warning the first time (and after each app restart). You tap through it once and you're in
  • Uses a self-signed certificate — your PC generates its own certificate. It's perfectly safe (everything stays on your network), but your browser doesn't recognize the issuer, so it shows a warning

Tailscale — Port 3457 (Trusted Certificate)

  • Only available if Tailscale is installed and configured on both your PC and phone (free, ~10 min setup)
  • Works from anywhere — different WiFi, mobile data, even different countries. Your phone connects to your PC through Tailscale's private network
  • No browser warnings ever — Dictator gets a real, trusted certificate from Let's Encrypt via Tailscale. Your browser treats it like any normal website
  • No port forwarding or firewall changes needed for the Tailscale connection

Which should I use? Start with LAN — it works immediately with zero setup. If you find yourself wanting to dictate from outside your home network, or you're tired of tapping through the browser warning, set up Tailscale. You can always use both — they run side by side.

Quick Start Option A: Accept the Self-Signed Certificate

Dictator automatically creates its own certificate when it starts. Your phone's browser doesn't recognize it (because it wasn't issued by a well-known authority), so it shows a warning. You just need to tell your browser it's OK.

iPhone (Safari)

1
Open Safari on your iPhone and go to the URL shown in the Dictator desktop app (e.g. https://192.168.1.100:3456)
2
You'll see "This Connection Is Not Private" — this is expected!
3
Tap "Show Details"
4
Tap "visit this website"
5
Tap "Visit Website" to confirm
6
The Dictator page loads. When Safari asks to use your microphone, tap "Allow"
7
You're done! Tap the mic button and start dictating.

Note: You may need to accept the warning again if you restart the Dictator app (it generates a new certificate each time it starts). This is normal.

Android (Chrome)

1
Open Chrome and go to the URL shown in the Dictator desktop app
2
You'll see "Your connection is not private"
3
Tap "Advanced"
4
Tap "Proceed to [address] (unsafe)"
5
Allow microphone access when prompted. Done!

Recommended Option B: Use Tailscale (No Warnings Ever)

Tailscale is a free app that creates a private, secure connection between your devices. When both your PC and phone are on Tailscale, Dictator gets a real, trusted certificate — just like any normal HTTPS website. No browser warnings, no extra steps on your phone.

What is Tailscale?

Think of it as a virtual cable between your devices. It works over any network (home WiFi, mobile data, coffee shop WiFi) and everything is encrypted. It's made by a well-known company and is free for personal use (up to 100 devices).

Setup Steps

One-time setup (about 10 minutes)

1
Install Tailscale on your PC
Go to tailscale.com and download the Windows app. Sign in with your Google, Microsoft, or Apple account.
2
Install Tailscale on your phone
Get "Tailscale" from the App Store (iPhone) or Play Store (Android). Sign in with the same account.
3
Enable HTTPS certificates
Open a browser and go to login.tailscale.com/admin/dns. Scroll down to "HTTPS Certificates" and click "Enable HTTPS".
4
Restart Dictator
Close and reopen the desktop app. It will detect Tailscale automatically and get a trusted certificate.
5
Connect your phone
Open the new URL shown in the desktop app (it will look like https://your-pc.your-tailnet.ts.net:3457). No warnings — the mic works immediately!

Why Tailscale is worth it: Once set up, your phone connects to your PC from anywhere — even when you're on mobile data or a different WiFi network. No port forwarding, no firewall rules, and the certificate is trusted by every browser.

Troubleshooting

My phone says "can't reach this page" or "connection refused"
Make sure your phone and PC are on the same WiFi network. Also check that Windows Firewall allows ports 3456 (LAN) and 3457 (Tailscale). You can add inbound rules manually in Windows Firewall settings.
Safari shows "This Connection Is Not Private" and I can't get past it
Tap "Show Details" at the bottom of the warning page, then "visit this website". If you don't see these options, make sure you're using Safari (not Chrome on iPhone — Chrome on iOS may not let you proceed past the warning). Consider setting up Tailscale for a permanent fix.
The page loads but the mic button doesn't work
Check that you allowed microphone access in your browser. On iPhone: Settings > Safari > Microphone should be set to "Ask" or "Allow". Then reload the Dictator page and tap "Allow" when prompted.
Tailscale is installed but Dictator still shows "Self-signed"
Make sure you completed Step 3 above (enable HTTPS in the Tailscale admin panel). Also make sure Tailscale is connected (look for the Tailscale icon in your system tray — it should say "Connected"). Then restart Dictator.
Dictator says "Tailscale cert failed"
The tailscale cert command may need administrator access. Try running Dictator as administrator (right-click > Run as administrator), or generate the certificate manually: open Command Prompt as Administrator and run:
tailscale cert --cert-file=%TEMP%\dictator-certs\cert.pem --key-file=%TEMP%\dictator-certs\key.pem YOUR-MACHINE.YOUR-TAILNET.ts.net
(Replace with your actual Tailscale domain shown in the Dictator app.)
Can I use this over the internet, not just my home network?
Yes! With Tailscale, your phone can connect to your PC from anywhere — different WiFi network, mobile data, even a different country. That's one of the biggest advantages of Tailscale.
Is the self-signed certificate safe?
Yes. The certificate is generated locally on your PC and is only used to encrypt the connection between your phone and PC on your own network. No data leaves your network. The browser warning exists because your browser doesn't know who created the certificate, not because anything is wrong.

GPU Acceleration

Loading GPU info...

Dictator uses Whisper to convert your speech to text. By default it runs on your CPU, which works fine. If you have a dedicated graphics card (GPU), you can speed up transcription significantly.

How to Enable GPU

GPU acceleration uses Vulkan, which works with NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel GPUs. To build Dictator with GPU support:

  1. Install the Vulkan SDK from vulkan.lunarg.com — run the installer and accept defaults
  2. Rebuild Dictator using the GPU build command:
    build-gpu.bat
  3. Dictator will automatically use your GPU if available, and fall back to CPU if anything goes wrong

Most users don't need this. The base English model is small and runs fast on modern CPUs. GPU acceleration shines if you notice a delay between stopping your recording and seeing the text, or if you switch to a larger model for better accuracy.
